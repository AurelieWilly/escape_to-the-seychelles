Description

The Seychelles archipelago consists of 115 islands: 41 granitic islands & 74 coral islands, lying between the east coast of Africa in the western Indian Ocean. The Seychelles represents an archipelago of timeless beauty, tranquillity and harmony and famous for its worldbeating beaches and for its great diversity which rolls from lush forests down to the warm azure ocean.
The archipelag is divided into 41 Inner Islands that constitutes the oldest midoceanic granitic islands on Earth while a further 74 form the 5 groups of low-lying coral atolls and reef islets that are the Outer Islands.
For the population, there is approximately 86000 inhabitants, most of whom live on the main islands of Mahé, Praslin and La Digue. The Seychellois are a mix of the European, African and Asiatic races. They speak Creole, English and French.

What to bring along?

Bathing costume and light cotton clothes. Most hotels insist on men wearing slacks in the evening and a light, cotton jumper is also ideal for the cool evenings and against mosquitoes. Plastic sandals are very practical when exploring the coral reefs. But most importantly solar cream, masks, tubas and beach towels are indispensable to have pleasant stay in Seychelles...
